Evolution of Chinese Characters

Chinese characters are an ancient writing system dating back to initial symbols carved into pottery and bone 8,000 years ago. It evolved over thousands of years into a unified system 3,000 years ago, and has evolved further into its modern form.

Unlike Western alphabets, Chinese characters are logographic, using symbols to represent words or phrases. The progression from primitive inscriptions to today's script reflects a complex development from ancient oracle bones script to today’s modern writing style.

Chinese characters went through 6 stages of evolution:

Oracle Bone Script, Bronze Script, Small Seal Script, Clerical Script, Regular Script, Simplified Chinese Script.
